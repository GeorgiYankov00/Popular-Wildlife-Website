The Most Popular Wildlife Animals

Panda cub

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Class: Mammalia
  • Order: Carnivora
  • Family: Ursidae
  • Status: Vulnerable

The giant panda also known as the panda bear or simply the panda, is a bear native to south central China. It is characterised by large, black patches around its eyes, over the ears, and across its round body. The giant panda lives in a few mountain ranges in central China, mainly in Sichuan, but also in neighbouring Shaanxi and Gansu. For more interesting facts visit here!

Clownfish

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Class: Actinopterygii
  • Family: Pomacentridae
  • Subfamily: Amphiprioninae
  • Status: NaN

Clownfish or anemonefish are fishes from the subfamily Amphiprioninae in the family Pomacentridae. Thirty species are recognized. Depending on species, anemonefish are overall yellow, orange, or a reddish or blackish color, and many show white bars or patches. The largest can reach a length of 17 cm. For more interesting facts visit here!

Macaw

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Class: Aves
  • Order: Psittaciformes
  • Family: Psittacidae
  • Status: Vulnerable

The hyacinth macaw (Anodorhynchus hyacinthinus), or hyacinthine macaw, is a parrot native to central and eastern South America. With a length (from the top of its head to the tip of its long pointed tail) of about one meter (3.3 ft) it is longer than any other species of parrot. It is the largest macaw and the largest flying parrot species. For more interesting facts visit here!

Pandas are sweet and lazy

As a result of farming, deforestation, and other development, the giant panda has been driven out of the lowland areas were it once lived, and it is a conservation-reliant vulnerable species. Wild population estimates vary; one estimate shows that there are about 1,590 individuals living in the wild

Reefs are the home for most tropical fishes

Tropical coral reefs are the most diverse marine ecosystems on earth, giving shelter to thousands of animal species. Yet today, coral reefs are dying at an alarming rate all around the globe.The impact of our changing climate on coral reefs was manifested by the third global bleaching event in 2015/16. This event has caused a mass die-off of corals.
